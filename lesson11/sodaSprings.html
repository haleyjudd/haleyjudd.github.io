Idaho's Climate

Idaho’s mountainous topography produces an diverse climate pattern. In general, as elevation increases, precipitation increases and mean temperatures drop. On a different scale, the high mountains in eastern Idaho tend to hold back the cold winter air that predominates in Montana and Wyoming, giving the state higher mean temperatures than are found at the same latitude and elevation in those states and in more mid-continental locations. Mean January temperatures range from the upper 10s F (about −8 °C) at Deadwood Dam in the central mountains to the low 30s F (about −1 °C) at Orofino in the central panhandle. July temperatures range from about 60 °F (16 °C) at Deadwood Dam to the upper 70s F (about 25 °C) at Grand View in the southwest. https://www.britannica.com/place/Idaho
